Flatbread recipe

Illustration by Gemini Nano Banana Pro

Description

Flatbread was a daily staple of the Viking diet, valued for its extreme portability, long shelf life, and ease of preparation. In the Norse era, bread was rarely leavened with yeast; instead, it was made by mixing ground grains with water or soured milk into a dense dough. Because wheat was difficult to grow in the harsh Scandinavian climate, Vikings relied heavily on hardier crops like barley, rye, and spelt, which gave their everyday bread a dense texture and a robust, nutty flavor profile.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup barley flour (or spelt flour)
  • 1 cup rye flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 3/4 cup water (or buttermilk/sour milk)
  • 1 tbsp melted butter or lard (optional, for authentic richness)

Steps

  1. Mix the Dough: In a large bowl, whisk together the barley flour, rye flour, and salt. Pour in the water (or buttermilk) and melted fat, stirring until a firm dough forms.
  2. Knead: Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead it for a few minutes until it is smooth and cohesive. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour; if it is too crumbly, add a teaspoon of water.
  3. Rest: Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15 to 20 minutes to allow the coarse grains to hydrate.
  4. Portion and Roll: Divide the dough into 4 to 6 equal portions. Roll each piece into a small ball, then flatten it with a rolling pin (or your hands) into a thin round, roughly 1/8-inch thick.
  5. Cook: Heat a heavy cast-iron skillet or griddle over medium-high heat without any oil. Place a flatbread in the dry pan and cook for 2 to 3 minutes until brown spots appear on the bottom. Flip and cook the other side for another 1 to 2 minutes.
  6. Serve: Stack the cooked flatbreads under a clean towel to keep them warm and pliable, and serve alongside stews or broth.
