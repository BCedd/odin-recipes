Wholegrain barley porridge recipe

Illustration by Gemini Nano Banana Pro

Description

Wholegrain barley porridge that has been a staple in Nordic countries since prehistoric times. Because barley is one of Norway's most abundantly grown grains, this porridge was historically eaten several times a day. It is highly regarded for being nutritious, filling, and warming—especially ideal for those working long, demanding hours in cold climates.

The recipe is simple and deeply rooted in ancient traditions. While it is inherently wholesome on its own, it is highly versatile and typically served with comforting toppings. Traditional garnishes include melted butter, cinnamon, and sugar, though modern variations often incorporate fresh sliced fruit or raisins.

Ingredients

  • (9 oz) whole pearl barley
  • 500ml (16 fl oz) cold water
  • 600ml (20 fl oz) milk
  • Pinch of salt
  • Optional toppings: Melted butter, sugar, cinnamon, sliced fruit

Steps

  1. Soak the Barley: Soak the barley overnight in a bowl with plenty of fresh water.
  2. Boil the Barley: The following day, drain the barley and discard the soaking water. Place the soaked grains and the 500ml of cold water into a large pan. Cover with a lid and slowly bring to a boil.
  3. Simmer in Water: Reduce the heat to very low and let it simmer for 30 minutes, or until the barley is just tender and most of the water has been absorbed.
  4. Simmer in Milk: Add the milk to the pan and simmer again for about 45 minutes until you achieve a thick porridge consistency. Stir the mixture occasionally to prevent the barley from sticking to the bottom of the pan.
  5. Rest: Remove the pan from the heat, replace the lid, and allow the porridge to stand undisturbed for 10 minutes.
  6. Serve: Serve warm or hot, ideally drizzled with melted butter and dusted with sugar and cinnamon to taste.
